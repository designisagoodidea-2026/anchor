**Rule:** Don't use the words below in any output drafted in Jason's voice. Substitute with the alternatives suggested, or restructure the sentence to avoid the concept entirely. When the underlying concept is genuinely useful, reach for a plainer word.

**Why:** Established 2026-05-28. Jason's exact words: *"There are a few key words that I really prefer to avoid... I'm sure there are other keywords that I prefer to avoid."* This is a growing list — add to it as he flags new ones during drafting cycles, and document the reasoning so the avoidance is principled rather than mechanical.

## The list (with reasoning)

### modality
**Why disliked:** sounds pretentious. Common in design/AI/HCI jargon ("multi-modal", "the spatial modality") but reads as performative vocabulary in plain writing.

**Substitutes:**
- For XR / spatial computing / new interaction paradigms → "kind of computing", "form of computing", "form factor", "the work", "the field"
- For "multi-modal research" → "research across formats", "research using multiple methods" (depending on what was actually done)
- Best move: name the actual thing. "Spatial computing" instead of "the spatial modality." "Voice + touch" instead of "multi-modal."

### scale (as verb and as noun in growth contexts)
**Why disliked:** grossly overused. Most people who say "scale" actually mean "growth," and growth is *linear* — it doesn't have the *logarithmic character* that real scaling has. The word inflates ordinary growth into something it isn't.

**Substitutes:**
- For team/org growth → "grew", "grow", "growth", "growing"
- For volume increase → "more X", "expanding", "broader reach"
- For real scaling (logarithmic, non-linear, infrastructure-driven) → "scale" is acceptable IF the work actually had that character. Be honest about it.

**Examples (bad → good):**
- "scaled the team from 4 to 12" → "grew the team from 4 to 12"
- "operationalize at scale" → "operationalize across the team" / "operationalize across 30+ designers"
- "design at scale" → "design across [the actual surface area]"

### process (in design contexts)
**Why disliked:** Jason believes most design is *practice*, not *process*. The distinction matters: process is repeatable steps; practice is judgment exercised under conditions. Calling design "process" reduces it to mechanical execution.

**Substitutes:**
- "design process" → "design practice", "the design work", "how the team works"
- "process improvement" (in design contexts) → "practice refinement", "how the team operates"
- "operating process" → "operating practice", "operating practices"

**Tied to Jason's first operating value:** [[cos-values]] "Practice Before Process" — *practice even over process*. Curiosity, sense-making, and critical thinking create more value than checklists. Heavy templates and RACI matrices replace human discernment with paperwork. The word "process" carries the paperwork connotation Jason is explicitly against.

**Edge cases:** when describing a non-design process where the word is genuinely accurate (e.g., a hiring *process*, an onboarding *process*, a regulated workflow), the word is fine. The avoidance is specifically about design work being mis-categorized as process.

## Likely future additions (watch for these — confirm with Jason before adding)

These are pattern-matches for the kind of overused/pretentious vocabulary Jason has flagged. If they show up in drafts, consider whether to flag for him:

- "leverage" (as verb) — corporate filler; substitute "use", "apply", "draw on"
- "synergy" / "synergize" — obvious filler
- "stakeholder alignment" (without specifying which stakeholders) — vague
- "best-in-class" — already on the [[cover-letter]] forbidden adjectives list
- "innovative" / "passionate" / "results-driven" / "strategic thinker" / "frictionless" — already on the [[cover-letter]] forbidden adjectives list
- "ecosystem" (corporate jargon usage, not biological/literal)
- "robust" (as a substitute for "good" or "thorough")
- "deliverables" — corporate filler; substitute "outputs", "work product", or name the actual thing
- "stand up" (a team, a function, a practice) — startup-y filler; substitute "build", "establish", "set up"

These are NOT yet confirmed-disliked — flag if they appear and ask Jason if he wants them added.

## How to apply

1. **At drafting time:** check every sentence for the confirmed banned words above. Substitute or restructure.
2. **At review time:** if the draft has one of the "likely future additions," surface to Jason in the chat callout — "I used 'leverage' in para 3; should I rewrite?" — don't sneak it through.
3. **When Jason flags a new word:** add to the confirmed list above with the reasoning he gives. The reasoning matters as much as the word.
4. **When in doubt about a synonym:** pick the plainer word. Jason's voice (per [[cos-values]] "Voice and register") is *direct without being curt, concrete without being clinical*. Plain words serve that voice.
