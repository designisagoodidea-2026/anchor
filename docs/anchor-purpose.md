## Purpose

Anchor is an MCP-based operating-sense system for design and product leaders running embedded teams. It ingests change signal from the tools a team actually uses (authoring tools, AI workspaces, communication channels, scheduling), resolves that signal to the projects and bodies of work it belongs to, translates activity into leadable signals (capacity, health, drift, decision rework), and renders the result as an anchored narrative in the leader's preferred voice — here's what was true the last time you read this, here's what's moved, here's the question worth asking.

Not a dashboard. A report a leader will read.

## Differentiation

Every operations-reporting tool on the market today renders state. Anchor renders state-change in the leader's voice, anchored against what the leader said last time. The continuity is the load-bearing differentiator. Three orthogonal layers carry it:

- **MCP bus** — tool-set-agnostic.
- **Leader-defined principles layer** — culture-aware.
- **Voice profile layer** — craft-aware.

No existing tool combines all three. The combination is the defensibility argument.

## Audience

- **First user**: Jason — building it for himself first; then for senior design and DPM leaders running embedded teams across business units.
- **Second user**: the leader running an embedded function (Head of Design Ops, VP Design, Head of Design Strategy) at a compound-shaped organization.
- **Third user**: any team lead in a tool-heavy creative or product org who wants signal without survey.

The ordering matters for build priorities. The first user being Jason means the POC ships against his tool surface, his voice, his principles. The second shapes the signal taxonomy. The third is the scaled-state hypothesis; future, not present.

## Why Jason is the right builder

Two decades running design ops at large product organizations. Has felt the dashboard-doesn't-work problem from inside multiple operating models. Has published the underlying architecture — capacity inference, organic adoption, lightweight governance. Has lived the case where an embedded function was structurally undone because the cross-cutting value wasn't legible at leadership level. Anchor exists to make that value legible while there's still time.

The Personal Chief of Staff system is the proof Jason can build and ship AI-first operational infrastructure end-to-end. Anchor is the externalized form of that capability.
